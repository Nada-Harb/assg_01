Exotic dance moves

Yes, you are right! As part of my post-doctorate work, I did study exotic tribal dances. To answer your question,
my favourite dances are as follows, with definitions:

Polynesian chicken dance

A little known but very influential dace back as far as 300BC , a whole village would dance around
in a circle like chickens, to encourage their livestock to be " fruitful "

Icelandic brownian shuffle

Befor the Icelandic developed fire as a means of gitting warm, they used to practice this dancec, which
involved huddling close together in a circle on the floor, and shuffling their bodies around in imperceptibly
tiny,very rapid movements. One of my fellow studants used to say that he thought this dance inspired
modern styles such as Twerking.

Arctic robot dance

An interesting example of historic misinformation, English explorers in the 1960s belived to have
discovered a new dance style characterised by "robotic ", stilted movements, being practiced by inhabitants
of Northern Alaska and Canada. Later on however it was discovered that they were just moving like this
because they were really cold.
